Major Health Topics in Ethiopia

Malaria

Mosquito-borne parasitic disease.

Description: Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted through bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

Symptoms: Fever, chills, headache, sweating, nausea, vomiting, fatigue.

Prevention: Use insecticide-treated bed nets, indoor spraying, elimination of stagnant water.

Treatment: Antimalarial medicines provided at health facilities.

HIV & AIDS

Virus that weakens the immune system.

Description: HIV attacks the immune system and can lead to AIDS if untreated.

Symptoms: Weight loss, recurrent infections, fatigue, night sweats.

Prevention: Condom use, HIV testing, safe blood transfusion.

Treatment: Lifelong antiretroviral therapy (ART).

Tuberculosis (TB)

Airborne bacterial lung disease.

Description: TB is caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis and mainly affects the lungs.

Symptoms: Persistent cough, chest pain, weight loss, night sweats.

Prevention: Early diagnosis, BCG vaccination, good ventilation.

Treatment: Combination antibiotics for at least 6 months.

Cholera

Severe diarrheal disease.

Description: Cholera is an acute diarrheal infection caused by contaminated food or water.

Symptoms: Profuse watery diarrhea, dehydration, vomiting.

Prevention: Clean water, sanitation, handwashing.

Treatment: Oral rehydration solution (ORS), IV fluids.

COVID-19

Viral respiratory infection.

Description: COVID-19 is caused by SARS-CoV-2 virus.

Symptoms: Fever, cough, breathing difficulty, loss of taste.

Prevention: Vaccination, hand hygiene, masks.

Treatment: Supportive care, oxygen for severe cases.

Diabetes

High blood sugar disorder.

Description: Diabetes occurs when blood glucose remains high.

Symptoms: Excess thirst, frequent urination, fatigue.

Prevention: Healthy diet, physical activity.

Treatment: Insulin or oral medications.

Hypertension

High blood pressure.

Description: Hypertension increases risk of heart disease and stroke.

Symptoms: Often none, sometimes headache.

Prevention: Reduce salt, exercise, avoid smoking.

Treatment: Lifestyle changes and medication.

Asthma

Chronic lung disease.

Description: Asthma causes airway inflammation and narrowing.

Symptoms: Wheezing, coughing, breathlessness.

Prevention: Avoid triggers.

Treatment: Inhalers and medications.

Measles

Highly contagious viral disease.

Description: Measles spreads through air droplets.

Symptoms: Fever, rash, cough, red eyes.

Prevention: Vaccination.

Treatment: Supportive care.

Typhoid Fever

Bacterial foodborne disease.

Description: Caused by Salmonella typhi.

Symptoms: Prolonged fever, abdominal pain.

Prevention: Clean food and water.

Treatment: Antibiotics.

Hepatitis B

Viral liver infection.

Description: Hepatitis B is a viral infection that attacks the liver and can become chronic.

Symptoms: Jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain, dark urine.

Prevention: Vaccination, safe sex, screened blood.

Treatment: Antiviral medicines and monitoring.

Hepatitis C

Blood-borne liver disease.

Description: Hepatitis C causes liver inflammation and damage.

Symptoms: Fatigue, nausea, jaundice.

Prevention: Safe injections, screened blood.

Treatment: Antiviral therapy.

Breast Cancer

Cancer affecting breast tissue.

Description: Uncontrolled growth of breast cells.

Symptoms: Lump, nipple discharge, breast changes.

Prevention: Screening, healthy lifestyle.

Treatment: Surgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy.

Cervical Cancer

Cancer of the cervix.

Description: Caused mainly by HPV infection.

Symptoms: Abnormal vaginal bleeding.

Prevention: HPV vaccination, screening.

Treatment: Surgery, radiotherapy.

Prostate Cancer

Male reproductive cancer.

Description: Cancer of the prostate gland.

Symptoms: Urinary difficulty.

Prevention: Regular screening.

Treatment: Surgery, radiotherapy.

Mental Health

Psychological well-being.

Description: Emotional and psychological health.

Symptoms: Anxiety, mood changes.

Prevention: Social support, stress management.

Treatment: Counseling, medication.

Depression

Mood disorder.

Description: Persistent feeling of sadness.

Symptoms: Loss of interest, fatigue.

Prevention: Social support.

Treatment: Therapy, antidepressants.

Obesity

Excess body fat.

Description: Abnormal fat accumulation.

Symptoms: Breathlessness, fatigue.

Prevention: Balanced diet, exercise.

Treatment: Lifestyle changes.

Malnutrition

Nutrient deficiency.

Description: Lack of essential nutrients.

Symptoms: Weight loss, weakness.

Prevention: Balanced nutrition.

Treatment: Nutritional supplementation.

Diarrheal Diseases

Frequent loose stools.

Description: Caused by infections.

Symptoms: Diarrhea, dehydration.

Prevention: Clean water.

Treatment: ORS, zinc.

Rabies

Fatal viral disease.

Description: Spread through animal bites.

Symptoms: Fever, hydrophobia.

Prevention: Vaccination.

Treatment: Post-exposure prophylaxis.

Leprosy

Chronic bacterial disease.

Description: Affects skin and nerves.

Symptoms: Skin patches, numbness.

Prevention: Early treatment.

Treatment: Multi-drug therapy.

Epilepsy

Neurological disorder.

Description: Causes recurrent seizures.

Symptoms: Seizures, confusion.

Prevention: Injury prevention.

Treatment: Anti-epileptic drugs.

Stroke

Brain blood flow interruption.

Description: Medical emergency.

Symptoms: Paralysis, speech problems.

Prevention: Control BP.

Treatment: Emergency care.

Heart Disease

Cardiovascular disorders.

Description: Affects heart function.

Symptoms: Chest pain.

Prevention: Healthy lifestyle.

Treatment: Medication, surgery.

Kidney Disease

Impaired kidney function.

Description: Chronic kidney disease reduces the kidneys’ ability to filter waste.

Symptoms: Swelling, fatigue, reduced urine.

Prevention: Control diabetes and blood pressure.

Treatment: Medication, dialysis, transplant.

Eye Diseases

Visual impairment conditions.

Description: Includes cataract, glaucoma, and infections.

Symptoms: Blurred vision, eye pain.

Prevention: Regular eye checks.

Treatment: Medication or surgery.

Dental Health

Oral and tooth health.

Description: Poor oral hygiene leads to cavities and gum disease.

Symptoms: Tooth pain, bleeding gums.

Prevention: Brushing, flossing.

Treatment: Dental care and treatment.

Skin Infections

Bacterial, fungal, or viral skin diseases.

Description: Infections affecting the skin layers.

Symptoms: Rash, itching, redness.

Prevention: Hygiene.

Treatment: Antimicrobial medicines.

Pneumonia

Lung infection.

Description: Infection that inflames air sacs in lungs.

Symptoms: Cough, fever, breathing difficulty.

Prevention: Vaccination.

Treatment: Antibiotics or antivirals.

Influenza

Seasonal flu virus.

Description: Viral respiratory infection.

Symptoms: Fever, body aches.

Prevention: Vaccination.

Treatment: Rest, fluids, antivirals.

COVID-19 Long Effects

Description: Persistent symptoms after COVID recovery.

Symptoms: Fatigue, brain fog.

Prevention: Vaccination.

Treatment: Symptom management.

Polio

Viral paralysis disease.

Description: Polio affects nerves and can cause paralysis.

Symptoms: Muscle weakness.

Prevention: Vaccination.

Treatment: Supportive care.

Schistosomiasis

Parasitic waterborne disease.

Description: Caused by parasites in freshwater.

Symptoms: Abdominal pain, blood in urine.

Prevention: Avoid unsafe water.

Treatment: Praziquantel.

Onchocerciasis

Description: Parasitic disease spread by blackflies.

Symptoms: Itching, vision loss.

Prevention: Vector control.

Treatment: Ivermectin.

Sleeping Sickness

Description: Parasitic disease affecting the brain.

Symptoms: Sleep disturbance.

Prevention: Avoid tsetse flies.

Treatment: Antiparasitic drugs.

Leishmaniasis

Prevention: Bed nets.

Treatment: Antiparasitic drugs.

Symptoms: Fever, bleeding.
