Malaria: Symptoms, Prevention & Treatment

Malaria is a serious disease caused by a parasite transmitted through the bites of infected mosquitoes. It remains a major public health problem in Ethiopia, especially in lowland and rural areas.

  • Fever and chills
  • Headache and fatigue
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • In severe cases: anemia, confusion, or coma

ü¶ü How Malaria Spreads

Malaria spreads when a person is bitten by a female *Anopheles* mosquito infected with the malaria parasite (*Plasmodium*). The parasites then travel to the liver, multiply, and infect red blood cells.

‚úÖ Prevention Tips

  • Sleep under insecticide-treated bed nets every night.
  • Drain standing water where mosquitoes breed.
  • Use mosquito repellents or coils at night.
  • Wear long-sleeved clothing during evening hours.
  • Take preventive medication if advised by a health worker.

If you suspect malaria, visit the nearest health center for a **blood test**. Treatment depends on the type of malaria parasite. Common drugs include *Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT)*. Do not self-medicate ‚Äî always consult a doctor or health worker.
